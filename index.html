Soumya (Shomo) Kundu

Ph.D. Student at CAI4CAI (King's College London) supervised by Tom Vercauteren and Jonathan Shapey. My thesis is on applied machine learning for neuro-oncology. Automatically segmenting brain tumuors was truly mesmerising as an undergrad. So I am continuing to do so now as well. I am fully funded by the Medical Research Council.

My general interest is in medical image computing, and figuring out how to do it securely, efficiently, and transparently. Good labels are tough to come by. Even tougher when it involves the human brain. So, I also like self-supervised learning.

For my M.Sc., I was at the DERI (Queen Mary Univeristy of London) advised by Greg Slabaugh and Vineet Batta. I have had a few fantastic summers interning at Oxford (with Bartek Papiez), TRIUMF (with Patrick de Perio and Akira Konaka) and IIT-Gandhinagar (with Tarun Gangwar).

In my past life, I represented my country, state (national silver medal) and (captained) school in futsal.
